Lesson 1.3: The T-Account System

A T-account is a visual tool used in double-entry bookkeeping to track financial transactions. It has the account title at the top, debits on the left, and credits on the right. It looks like the letter T. Each transaction must have an equal amount of debits and credits in at least 2 accounts.

Standard Format
CASH ACCOUNT / LEDGER VIEW
LEFT SIDE
DEBIT
(Dr.)
RIGHT SIDE
CREDIT
(Cr.)

The "DEALER" Mnemonic

The easiest way to remember which accounts increase with debits versus credits is the acronym DEALER:

D E A Increases with Debits
  • Dividends (Increases with a debit)
  • Expenses (Increases with a debit)
  • Assets (Increases with a debit)
L E R Increases with Credits
  • Liabilities (Increases with a credit)
  • Equity (Increases with a credit)
  • Revenue (Increases with a credit)
