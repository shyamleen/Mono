The Absolute Accounting Rule of Debits and Credits

You must completely forget everyday bank terms, where "debiting" a card feels like a reduction. In accounting, "Debit simply means Left, and Credit simply means Right." They are directional instructions, not mathematical symbols. Whether a debit increases or decreases an account depends entirely on what type of account it is.

  • Left-Side Equation Accounts (Assets): Increased on their natural side—the left (Debit). Decreased on the opposite side—the right (Credit).
  • Right-Side Equation Accounts (Liabilities & Equity): Increased on their natural side—the right (Credit). Decreased on the opposite side—the left (Debit).

Determining Account Balances

To find the formal closing balance of a T-account at the end of an accounting period, you follow a strict formula:

  1. Sum the total dollar values listed in the Left (Debit) column.
  2. Sum the total dollar values listed in the Right (Credit) column.
  3. Subtract the smaller total from the larger total.
  4. Write the remaining difference on the side that had the larger initial sum. This number is your ending account balance.
