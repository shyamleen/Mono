Depreciation and Amortization

Depreciation and amortization are accounting methods used to spread the acquisition cost of an asset systematically across its expected useful lifespan. The operational dividing baseline depends completely on the core properties of the asset class.

⚙️ Depreciation: Tangible Assets

Accounts for the physical wear, operational aging, or direct technical obsolescence of physical property blocks.

Examples: Vehicles, heavy machinery, buildings, corporate office furniture.

* Often retains residual resale salvage values.

📜 Amortization: Intangible Assets

Spreads structural historical balance metrics for non-physical assets that yield legal long-term economic utility values.

Examples: Registered patents, trademarks, corporate copyrights, software.

* Typically holds zero end salvage value profiles.
