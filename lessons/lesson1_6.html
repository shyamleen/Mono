While the General Journal serves as a reliable chronological log of daily transactions, it has a major limitation: it doesn't give you instant visibility into individual accounts. If management wants to know the company's real-time cash balance, flipping through thousands of sequential journal entries to calculate the running total would be incredibly inefficient. This is why we use the General Ledger.

What is the General Ledger?

The General Ledger functions as a well-organized financial library. In this framework, every account (such as Cash, Inventory, and Equipment) has its own dedicated record page. Posting is the mechanical process of transferring data from the chronological General Journal into these specific account ledger pages.

The Five-Step Posting Routine

Posting requires no new analysis; it is a systematic data-entry routine designed to build a reliable financial database:

  1. Locate the Account: Find the specific account's dedicated record page in the general ledger.
  2. Transfer the Date: Enter the exact transaction date from the journal line entry.
  3. Transfer the Value: Copy the exact dollar amount into the identical column. Journal debits become ledger debits, and journal credits become ledger credits.
  4. Update the Running Balance: Calculate the new running total for that account immediately after posting the entry.
  5. Record the Posting Reference (Post. Ref.): Enter the cross-reference code (like J1 for Journal Page 1) in both books to create an easily auditable paper trail.
