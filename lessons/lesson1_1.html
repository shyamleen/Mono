Lesson 1.1: Introduction to Corporate Tracking

Imagine launching an independent custom sneaker design workspace with your friends. You write down your plan, lease a commercial printer, buy blank structural shoes, purchase heavy industrial paints, and launch a storefront. After operating, how do you track if you are making money or just burning through gear?

What is Accounting?

Accounting operates uniformly as the "universal language of business." It is a systematically standardized data framework created to "measure, record, analyze, and classify" financial records across platforms.

The Operational Core Functions

📝

Chronological Recording

Aggregating all monetary transfers, invoicing, resource acquisitions, and expenditure values immediately at source entry.

📊

Systematic Summarization

Classifying diverse corporate data metrics into clear, audit-ready structures known globally as financial statements.
