What is accounting?

Accounting is known as the language of business. In simple terms, It’s a framework that allows us to measure, record, analyze, and classify financial information.

What happens in an accounting cycle that helps us run a business?

The accounting cycle is a standardized, step-by-step process used to capture and organize financial data. Here is the lifecycle of every transaction:

1
Identify Transactions: Locate and analyze economic events, such as sales, vendor payments, or receipt of invoices.
2
Record in a Journal: Log transactions chronologically using double-entry bookkeeping, ensuring every debit has a matching credit.
3
Post to the General Ledger: Transfer journal entries into specific accounts to maintain a categorized record of all activity.
4
Prepare Unadjusted Trial Balance: Summarize the ledger at the end of the period to verify that total debits equal total credits.
5
Analyze the Worksheet & Identify Adjustments: Evaluate the trial balance to spot errors or missing entries.
6
Record Adjusting Journal Entries: Account for accruals, deferrals, and depreciation to align with core accounting principles.
7
Generate Financial Statements: Prepare primary financial reports—the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.
8
Close the Books: Transfer balances from temporary accounts (revenues and expenses) to permanent equity accounts, resetting the temporary accounts to zero for the next period.
