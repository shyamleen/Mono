Statement of Owner's Equity

The Statement of Owner's Equity documents exactly how the business owner's investment stake has changed over a specific operating cycle.

2. How to Make the Statement (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Write the Heading

Draft a three-line header consisting of the Business Name, the Statement Title, and the exact operating timeframe.

Step 2: Determine Beginning Equity

Locate the starting equity at day one of your timeline. If the business is brand new, this balance is $0.

Step 3: Add Investments and Net Income

Combine newly added capital contributions from the owner with Net Income derived directly from the Income Statement.

Step 4: Subtract Withdrawals & Calculate Ending Equity

Subtract personal owner withdrawals to calculate the final Ending Capital, which is then carried over to the Balance Sheet.
