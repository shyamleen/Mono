Statement of Owner's Equity

The Statement of Owner's Equity documents exactly how the business owner's investment stake has expanded or contracted over a specific operating cycle. It connects the profits from the Income Statement straight to the Balance Sheet.

2. How to Make the Statement (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Write the Heading

Draft a three-line header consisting of the Business Name, the Statement Title, and the exact operating timeframe.

Step 2: Determine Beginning Equity

Locate the initial starting equity at day one of your timeline. If the business is brand new, this start balance is $0.

Step 3: Add Investments and Net Income

Combine any newly added capital contributions from the owner with Net Income derived directly from the Income Statement.

Step 4: Subtract Withdrawals & Save Ending Equity

Subtract personal owner withdrawals (drawings) to calculate the final Ending Capital, then carry it over to the Balance Sheet.
