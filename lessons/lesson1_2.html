Lesson 1.2: The Core Accounting Equation

Every financial transaction impacts a company's fundamental balance scale equation. This relationship ensures that what you own matches exactly how you paid for it.

The Core Formula
Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity

1. Assets

What the business owns (by liquidity)

Current Assets
💵 Cash and Cash Equivalents ▼
Physical money and highly liquid short-term investments.
📝 Accounts Receivable (A/R) ▼
Money owed to the business by customers for goods or services sold on credit.
📦 Inventory ▼
Goods available for sale or materials used to produce goods.
⏳ Prepaid Expenses ▼
Advance payments for future services or expenses (e.g., prepaid rent or insurance).
Non-Current Assets
🏭 Property, Plant, and Equipment (PP&E) ▼
Physical assets like buildings, land, machinery, and vehicles.
💡 Intangible Assets ▼
Non-physical assets such as patents, trademarks, or goodwill.

2. Liabilities

What the business owes (by due dates)

Current Liabilities
🤝 Accounts Payable (A/P) ▼
Bills and money owed to suppliers or vendors for purchases made on credit.
🛑 Accrued Expenses ▼
Incurred expenses not yet paid or billed, such as wages payable or interest payable.
📥 Unearned/Deferred Revenue ▼
Money received in advance from customers for goods or services yet to be delivered.
📉 Short-Term Debt ▼
Loans or notes payable within a 12-month period.
Long-Term Liabilities
🏦 Long-Term Debt/Notes Payable ▼
Loans, mortgages, or bonds that stretch beyond a single year.

3. Equity

The owner's stake (residual value)

Equity Accounts
📈 Common Stock / Contributed Capital ▼
The amount invested directly into the business by its owners or shareholders.
💾 Retained Earnings ▼
The accumulated net income kept within the company rather than distributed to shareholders as dividends.
🔄 Treasury Stock ▼
The cost of the company's own stock that it has bought back from the open market.
👤 Owner's Capital ▼
The owner's personal investment in the business (for Sole Proprietorships/Partnerships).
💸 Owner's Draw ▼
Money withdrawn by the owner for personal use (for Sole Proprietorships/Partnerships).
