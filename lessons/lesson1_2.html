Lesson 1.2: The Core Accounting Equation

Every financial transaction impacts a company's fundamental balance scale equation. This relationship ensures that what you own matches exactly how you paid for it.

The Core Formula
Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity

1. Assets

What the business owns (by liquidity)

Current Assets
💵 Cash & Equivalents ▼
Physical money, checking balances, and highly liquid short-term investments.
📝 Accounts Receivable (A/R) ▼
Money owed to the business by customers for goods or services sold on credit terms.
📦 Inventory ▼
Finished goods available for sale, or materials used to assemble products.
⏳ Prepaid Expenses ▼
Advance payments made for services or expenses to be consumed in the future (e.g., insurance, rent).
Non-Current Assets
🏭 PP&E ▼
Property, Plant, & Equipment: Physical long-term items like structures, land, machinery, and trucks.
💡 Intangible Assets ▼
Non-physical assets carrying long-term balance utility, such as patents, codebases, or customer goodwill.

2. Liabilities

What the business owes (by due dates)

Current Liabilities
🤝 Accounts Payable (A/P) ▼
Bills and short-term obligations owed to commercial vendors or suppliers for credit purchases.
🛑 Accrued Expenses ▼
Incurred operational overhead not yet invoiced or processed, such as employee wages payable.
📥 Unearned Revenue ▼
Cash received ahead of project delivery; acts as an obligation to deliver products in the future.
📉 Short-Term Debt ▼
Active loans, lines of credit, or notes maturing within the upcoming 12-month operating timeframe.
Long-Term Liabilities
🏦 Long-Term Debt ▼
Long-term banking notes, mortgages, or issued bonds holding maturity limits greater than 1 year.

3. Equity

The owner's stake (residual value)

Equity Accounts
📈 Common Stock ▼
The total money directly paid into the business by external shareholders in exchange for ownership shares.
💾 Retained Earnings ▼
Cumulative historic net income generated by operations that is reinvested back into operations instead of paid out as dividends.
🔄 Treasury Stock ▼
A negative/contra equity account representing stock the company has bought back from the open market.
👤 Owner's Capital ▼
In partnerships or proprietorships, the personal monetary investment supplied directly by the founding partners.
💸 Owner's Draw ▼
Money withdrawn by the company owner for personal use in proprietorships and partnerships.
