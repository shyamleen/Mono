Lesson 1.6: Mastering Steps 1–4

Let's look at the 4 steps of accounting we have learned so far:

  • • Step 1 - Identify Transactions: Locate and analyze economic events, such as sales, vendor payments, or receipt of invoices.
  • • Step 2 - Record in a Journal: Log transactions chronologically using double-entry bookkeeping, ensuring every debit has a matching credit.
  • • Step 3 - Post to the General Ledger: Transfer journal entries into specific accounts to maintain a categorized record of all activity.
  • • Step 4 - Prepare Unadjusted Trial Balance: Summarize the ledger at the end of the period to verify that total debits equal total credits.
