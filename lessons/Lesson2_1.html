Introduction to Financial Statements

Financial statements are formal records that summarize a company’s financial positions over a specific period of time, such as a quarter or a year.

📋 The Four Core Financial Statements

⚖️

Balance Sheet

Provides a snapshot of a company's financial position at a single, specific point in time. It outlines what the business owns (Assets) and what it owes (Liabilities), with the remainder representing Shareholders' Equity.

📈

Income Statement

Also known as a Profit and Loss (P&L) statement, it details a company's revenues and expenses over a period. It calculates the final Net Income to show whether the business generated a profit or loss.

💧

Cash Flow Statement

Tracks the movement of cash in and out of a business across operating, investing, and financing activities. This confirms whether the company has enough liquid cash to pay its obligations.

👥

Shareholders' Equity Statement

Details the changes in the owners' stake in the business over the reporting period, including retained earnings and the issuance of stock.

📝

Important regulatory note:

These statements are made using GAAP or IFRS rules to ensure consistency and transparency.
