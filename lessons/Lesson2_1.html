Introduction to Financial Statements

Financial statements are formal records that summarize a company’s financial positions over a specific period of time, such as a quarter or a year.

The four core financial statements:

Balance Sheet: Provides a snapshot of a company's financial position at a single, specific point in time. It outlines what the business owns (Assets) and what it owes (Liabilities), with the remainder representing Shareholders' Equity.

Example: Balance Sheet Mockup
AssetsLiabilities & Equity
Cash: $50,000
Equipment: $100,000		Loans Payable: $60,000
Retained Earnings: $90,000
Total Assets: $150,000Total Liab. & Equity: $150,000

Income Statement: Also known as a Profit and Loss (P&L) statement, it details a company's revenues and expenses over a period. It calculates the final Net Income to show whether the business generated a profit or loss.

Example: Income Statement Mockup
Revenue (Sales)+$200,000
Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)-$80,000
Operating Expenses (Rent, Utilities)-$40,000
Net Income$80,000

Cash Flow Statement: Tracks the movement of cash in and out of a business across operating, investing, and financing activities. This confirms whether the company has enough liquid cash to pay its obligations.

Example: Cash Flow Statement Mockup
Cash from Operating Activities+$45,000
Cash from Investing Activities (Equipment Purchase)-$15,000
Cash from Financing Activities (Loan Repayment)-$10,000
Net Increase in Cash$20,000

Statement of Shareholders' Equity: Details the changes in the owners' stake in the business over the reporting period, including retained earnings and the issuance of stock.

Example: Shareholders' Equity Mockup
Beginning Equity (Jan 1)$70,000
Add: Net Income Contribution+$30,000
Less: Dividends Paid-$10,000
Ending Equity (Dec 31)$90,000

Important note: These statements are made using GAAP or IFRS rules to ensure consistency and transparency.
