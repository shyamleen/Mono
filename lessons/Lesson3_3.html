Lesson 3.3: Efficiency Ratios & Operational Velocity

Efficiency ratios (or activity ratios) measure how effectively a company uses its assets and manages liabilities to generate revenue. They highlight daily operational performance, showing whether resources are being maximized or if capital is needlessly tied up. Higher ratios generally signal strong performance, with optimal benchmarks varying by industry.

1. Inventory Turnover

Measures how many times a company sells and replaces its stock over a defined window.
Formula: Inventory Turnover = Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory

2. Receivables Turnover

Quantifies how quickly an enterprise collects outstanding cash balances from credit buyers.
Formula: Receivables Turnover = Net Credit Sales / Average Accounts Receivable

3. Asset Turnover

Evaluates top-line operational efficiency by assessing sales generated per dollar of asset.
Formula: Asset Turnover = Net Sales / Average Total Assets
