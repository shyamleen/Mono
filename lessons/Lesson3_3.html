Efficiency Ratios

Core Operational Concepts

Efficiency ratios (or activity ratios) measure how effectively a company uses its assets and manages liabilities to generate revenue. They highlight daily operational performance, showing whether resources are being maximized or if capital is needlessly tied up. Higher ratios generally signal strong performance, with optimal benchmarks varying by industry.

High-Efficiency Ratios

Signifies optimized workflows, fast inventory cycles, and quick customer cash collections.

Depressed-Efficiency Ratios

Indicates operating capital is locked away in unused assets or uncollected balances.
