The Income Statement

An income statement summarizes a company’s revenues, expenses, gains, and losses over a specific period. It begins with total revenue (the "top line") and ends with net income (the "bottom line").

2. How to Make an Income Statement (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Choose Header Info & Total Revenue

Set a standard three-line header. Record Gross Revenue and subtract returns to get your Top Line.

Step 2: Subtract Production Expenses (COGS)

Take your total revenue and subtract direct manufacturing costs.
Formula: Gross Profit = Total Revenue - COGS

Step 3: Account for Day-to-Day Operations

Deduct overhead costs like office rent, marketing, and wages.
Formula: Operating Income = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses

Step 4: The Bottom Line

Subtract non-operating charges to yield the final net earnings.
Formula: Net Income = Operating Income - Interest and Taxes
