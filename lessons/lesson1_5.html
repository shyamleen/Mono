Lesson 1.5: The Trial Balance

A Trial Balance is an internal report that lists the ending balances of every general ledger account to verify that your total debits equal your total credits.

How to Summarize Ledger Balances

  • Find Ending Balances: For each account in your general ledger, calculate the final balance by subtracting the smaller side from the larger side. (Example: If an asset has $5,000 in debits and $2,000 in credits, the ending balance is a $3,000 Debit.)
  • Sort by Type: Organize the balances into your trial balance worksheet using the standard accounting order: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenues, and Expenses.
  • Normal Balances (Columns):
    • Debit Column: Assets, Expenses, and Dividends.
    • Credit Column: Liabilities, Equity, and Revenue.
