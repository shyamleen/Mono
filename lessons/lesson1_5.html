Lesson 1.5: The Trial Balance

The Trial balance is a report that lists the ending balances of every general account to verify that total debits equal total credits.

How to Summarize Ledger Balances

Summarizing your ledger accounts is the first step in creating a trial balance.

🔍 1. Find Ending Balances For each account in your general ledger, calculate the final balance by subtracting the smaller side from the larger side.
Example: If an asset has $5,000 in debits and $2,000 in credits, the ending balance is a $3,000 Debit.
🗂️ 2. Sort by Type Organize the balances into your trial balance worksheet using the standard accounting order: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenues, and Expenses.
⚖️ Normal Balances:
🟢 Debit Column: Assets, Expenses, and Dividends.
🔴 Credit Column: Liabilities, Equity, and Revenue.

How to Check Your Math

➕ 1. Sum the Columns Add all values in the debit column to find the total debits. Repeat the process to find the total credits.
🔄 2. Compare Totals The total sum of the debit column must exactly match the total sum of the credit column.
⚠️ 3. Identify Discrepancies If the totals do not match, you have arithmetic or bookkeeping errors that must be investigated and resolved.
