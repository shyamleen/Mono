The Balance Sheet

The Balance Sheet acts as an instant snap-shot of what your business owns, what it owes, and how much is remaining for the investors. This structural layout always satisfies the legendary Accounting Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity.

2. How to Make a Balance Sheet (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Choose Date & Format

Set up an exact date (e.g. December 31, 2026). It must display financial accounts at that single point in time.

Step 2: List and Categorize Assets

List current assets (cash, A/R, inventory) followed by non-current fixed physical items (property, equipment).

Step 3: Organize Liabilities

Group debts by due date: current liabilities (due within a year) and long-term liabilities (due after a year).

Step 4: Review and Match Equation

Apply the calculation: Equity = Assets - Liabilities. Ensure Total Assets matches Total Liabilities + Total Equity perfectly!
