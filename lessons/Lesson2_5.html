The Cash Flow Statement

The cash flow statement tracks the movement of cash in and out of a business over a specific period. It reveals whether a business actually generated cash, regardless of credit transactions and accrual-based accounting records.

2. How to Make a Cash Flow Statement (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Calculate Operating Activities

Start with Net Income. Add back non-cash expenses like Depreciation, and adjust for changes in working capital (Inventory, Receivables, Payables).

Step 2: Calculate Investing Activities

Record cash used or received from long-term capital transactions, such as buying equipment, property, or selling investments.

Step 3: Account for Financing Activities

Track cash inflows from borrowing money or issuing stock, and outflows from paying off loans, repurchasing shares, or distributing dividends.

Step 4: Reconcile Cash Balances

Combine Operating, Investing, and Financing flows. Add the beginning cash balance to find the ending cash balance, which must match cash on the Balance Sheet.
