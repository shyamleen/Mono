Lesson 3.1: Working Capital & Liquidity Ratios

Working capital and liquidity ratios are critical metrics used to evaluate a company's short-term financial health and its ability to pay off immediate debts. They ensure smooth day-to-day operations and signal to investors whether a business is efficiently managing its cash and short-term assets.

1. Working Capital

Indicates the absolute amount of liquid cash a business has available for its daily operations.
Formula: Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities
Interpretation: Positive working capital means a company can easily fund operations. Negative suggest cash flow bottlenecks.

2. Current Ratio

Measures structural margin protection levels by checking total short asset coverage.
Formula: Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
Interpretation: Healthy ranges fall between 1.2 and 2.0. Below 1.0 flags short-term risk.

3. Quick Ratio (Acid-Test)

A strict test omitting complex inventory items that require conversion time.
Formula: Quick Ratio = (Cash + Marketable Securities + Accounts Receivable) / Current Liabilities
Interpretation: A value of 1.0 or higher is preferred, showing the ability to pay debts without selling stock.

4. Cash Ratio

The ultimate defense test checking immediate liquidity with pure monetary values.
Formula: Cash Ratio = (Cash + Cash Equivalents) / Current Liabilities
