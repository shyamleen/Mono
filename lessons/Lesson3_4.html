Lesson 3.4: Solvency Framework Analysis

A solvency ratio measures how well a company's cash flow can cover its long-term debt. Solvency ratios are a key metric for assessing the financial health of a company and can be used to determine the likelihood that a company will default on its debt.

1. Debt-to-Equity Ratio

Compares total balance liabilities straight against shareholder stakes to analyze financial leverage profiles.
Formula: Debt-to-Equity = Total Liabilities / Shareholders' Equity

2. Debt-to-Assets Ratio

Indicates the literal chunk size of structural equipment/property financed through debt capital.
Formula: Debt-to-Assets = Total Liabilities / Total Assets
Tip: Outcomes registering beyond 0.5 quickly flag highly aggressive reliance on capital leveraging.

3. Interest Coverage Ratio

Determines profit safety thresholds available to fulfill recurring interest obligations comfortably.
Formula: Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expenses

4. Equity Multiplier

Exposes asset funding magnification behaviors achieved via backing configurations.
Formula: Equity Multiplier = Total Assets / Shareholders' Equity
