M&A & Consolidation

Lesson 5.1: Business Combinations and Consolidations

In M&A and advanced accounting, business combinations are accounted for using the acquisition method, consolidating parent and subsidiary statements. All the subsidiary's assets and liabilities are reported at fair value. Goodwill represents the purchase premium paid above the fair value of net identifiable assets.

Calculating Goodwill

Goodwill is the residual value left over after determining the premium paid over the fair value of net identifiable assets.

Goodwill = (Consideration Transferred + Fair Value of NCI + Fair Value of Previous Equity) - Net Identifiable Assets
  • 1. Consideration Transferred: The total purchase price paid by the acquirer, which includes cash, stock issued, assumed liabilities, and contingent earn-outs.
  • 2. Net Identifiable Assets: The fair market value of the target's tangible assets and identifiable intangible assets (e.g., patents, brand names), minus its liabilities.
  • 3. Non-Controlling Interest (NCI): If the parent acquires less than 100% of the subsidiary, NCI must be accounted for.
  • 4. Fair Value Adjustments: The subsidiary's historical book values are adjusted upward or downward to reflect their exact market value on the acquisition date.

Parent and Subsidiary Consolidation Basics

Once the parent company gains controlling interest (typically holding more than 50% of voting stock), it must treat the parent and subsidiary as a single economic entity for financial reporting.

  • Full Consolidation: The parent combines 100% of the subsidiary’s revenues, expenses, assets, and liabilities on its consolidated balance sheet, regardless of its exact ownership percentage.
  • Elimination Entries: All intercompany transactions (e.g., intercompany sales, receivables, payables, dividends) must be fully eliminated to avoid double-counting at the group level.
  • Investment Elimination: The parent's "Investment in Subsidiary" account is replaced with the actual assets and liabilities of the subsidiary.

M&A Accounting Treatment

  • Acquisition Expenses: Costs like legal fees, consulting, and due diligence are expensed immediately in the period incurred.
  • Stock Issuance Costs: Costs directly associated with issuing new stock to finance the deal (e.g., underwriting fees) are deducted directly from Additional Paid-In Capital (APIC) rather than expensed.
  • Goodwill Impairment: Under US GAAP and IFRS, goodwill is not amortized. Instead, it must be evaluated for impairment annually to ensure its recorded value hasn't dropped.
