Lesson 3.2: Profitability Margins

Profitability margins are financial metrics used to assess a company's ability to turn sales into profit. Gross margin measures direct production efficiency, while net profit margin reveals overall bottom-line success after all expenses are paid. Both are typically expressed as percentages.

1. Gross Margin

Indicates profit made on products before factoring in general overhead, interest, and taxes.
Formula: Gross Margin = ((Revenue - COGS) / Revenue) * 100

2. Net Profit Margin

The comprehensive bottom line measure showing actual earnings conversion per sales dollar.
Formula: Net Profit Margin = (Net Income / Revenue) * 100
