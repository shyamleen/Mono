🗺️ Lesson 3.2: The Profitability Prism

Think of profitability metrics as an optical prism. Raw revenue enters through one side as clear white light. As it passes through the mechanics of your business, various operational friction layers break and absorb that light. What completely filters out determine your real financial margins.

🏳️‍🌈 Total Revenue
(Gross Sales)
➡️
🟢 Gross Margin
(Minus Production COGS)
➡️
🔮 Net Margin
(Minus All Expenses/Taxes)

1. Gross Profit Margin (The Value Filter)

Isolates the absolute production metrics and basic scale capabilities of your core product line, filtering out general administrative operations.

Gross Margin (%) = [(Revenue - COGS) / Revenue] × 100

2. Net Profit Margin (The Enterprise Filter)

The ultimate strategic corporate conversion standard. Out of every single dollar swiped by a retail consumer, how many pennies make it safely into the vault?

Net Profit Margin (%) = [Net Income / Revenue] × 100

📝 Prism Breakdown Check: Quiz 3.2

Complete your strategic evaluation challenge below:

Q2: A bakery generates $10,000 in weekly revenue. Ingredients and direct factory labor total $4,000. Find the Gross Margin percentage.

Excellent! Gross Profit = $10,000 - $4,000 = $6,000. Margin = ($6,000 / $10,000) × 100 = 60%.
