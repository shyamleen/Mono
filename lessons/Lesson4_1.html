Inventory Costing Methods

We measure inventory accounting to accurately calculate the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS), determine realistic profit margins, and value current assets on the balance sheet. It ensures legal compliance with GAAP or IFRS standards and prevents overstating company valuation.

Vital Functions of Inventory Measurement

  • Profitability Analysis: Tracks the exact expenses (materials, labor, overhead) tied to producing goods, allowing you to set profitable selling prices.
  • Financial Reporting: Values unsold goods as current assets to provide banks, investors, and lenders with an accurate picture of the company's financial health.
  • Tax Compliance: Reports inventory value correctly on tax returns.
  • Risk Mitigation: Identifies issues like obsolescence, spoilage, or shrinkage, preventing the inflation of assets and allowing for timely write-downs.

Specific Identification Method

Tracks the exact cost of each individual item. This is used primarily for high-ticket or unique items like luxury jewelry, real estate, or custom art, where mixing or smoothing costs would produce distorted balance sheets.
