Lesson 5.4: Revenue Recognition (ASC 606 & IFRS 15)

Both ASC 606 (US GAAP) and IFRS 15 require companies to follow a unified 5-step model to recognize revenue based on the transfer of control rather than cash receipt. While they share the core framework, nuances between the two frameworks remain.

The 5-Step Revenue Model

  • Identify the contract: Establish that a valid agreement exists (e.g., it has commercial substance and is legally enforceable).
  • Identify performance obligations: Unbundle the contract into distinct, separate promises to deliver goods or services.
  • Determine the transaction price: Calculate the amount the company expects to be entitled to, accounting for variable considerations.
  • Allocate the price: Distribute the total transaction price among the identified performance obligations based on their standalone selling prices.
  • Recognize revenue: Record the revenue when or as the control of the goods or services is transferred to the customer (either over time or at a point in time).

Key Differences Between US GAAP and IFRS

  • Collectibility Threshold: US GAAP (ASC 606) requires collection to be "probable" (generally interpreted as a 75%–80% chance). IFRS 15 sets the threshold lower at "more likely than not" (greater than 50%).
  • Shipping and Handling: ASC 606 offers a practical accounting policy election to treat shipping and handling activities that happen after the customer takes control as a simple fulfillment expense. IFRS 15 does not provide this election; these activities are strictly accounted for as a separate performance obligation.
  • Interim Disclosures: IFRS 15 enforces highly detailed interim (quarterly) disclosure requirements, including the disaggregation of revenue. ASC 606 provides more relaxed interim disclosure relief, particularly for private companies.
  • Onerous Contracts (Loss Contracts): Under IFRS 15, the measurement of a loss on an onerous contract defaults to the standard guidelines under IAS 37 (assessed at the contract level). US GAAP handles this on a contract-type specific basis within the Codification.
  • Business Combinations: Under US GAAP, acquired contract assets and liabilities are measured at their Topic 606 values. IFRS assesses them at their acquisition-date fair value.
